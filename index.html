Frequently Asked Questions

Does CabinPulse need WiFi to work?

No, WiFi is NOT required. CabinPulse uses cellular technology (LTE with 3G fallback) to transmit data, eliminating the need for WiFi. The device automatically connects to available cellular networks in your area.

Do I need to pay for another cellular plan?

No! The CabinPulse subscription covers everything. The cellular connectivity is completely built-in to the device, and there are no cellular data plans or WiFi required.

How do I setup my CabinPulse device?

Once you receive your device, login to your CabinPulse dashboard, enter the activation code on your device, and plug it into a power outlet. It will immediately begin monitoring!

My cabin has poor cell coverage - will CabinPulse work?

CabinPulse uses LTE with 3G fallback for areas without 3G; if there's any cell coverage, it will usually connect.


If you're unsure of coverage in your area, check the coverage maps from major carriers in your area for LTE or 3G.

What's included in the device fee?

The one-time $199 device fee covers your CabinPulse monitoring unit, which includes temperature, humidity, air quality, power outage detection capabilities and smoke alarm detection.

Can I buy multiple devices?

If you'd like to monitor multiple cabins or spaces, you can purchase additional devices for each location and manage them all from the same account using Fleet View. Each device requires its own subscription.

How up-to-date is the CabinPulse data?

CabinPulse collects data every 5 minutes and transmits every 15 minutes. You can also request real-time updates on-demand from your dashboard, and alerts for critical issues are sent instantly.

How are alerts sent?

You'll receive instant alerts via text and email for any critical issues detected by your CabinPulse device. You can customize the alert thresholds and choose which alerts you'd like to receive.

How many alerts can I configure?

You can set unlimited Standard Alerts, and set an Instant Alert for a high and low for each monitored metric.

What's the battery life of the CabinPulse device?

The CabinPulse device is designed to be plugged into a power outlet. However, it does have a backup battery that lasts up to 3 days in case of prolonged power outages.

How much electricity does CabinPulse use if it's plugged in 24/7?

The CabinPulse device is extremely energy efficient and uses about as much electricity as running your microwave for 60s - in an entire month!.

How does the alarm detection feature work?

An onboard acoustic sensor can recognize the distinct pattern of modern North American smoke alarms. Must be placed in close proximity to the detector and works best in an otherwise quiet environment.

How far back can I view historical data?

We store data captured from your device for as long as your subscription is active. If you choose to not renew your subscription, your data will be stored for up to 30 days before being deleted.

Can the device be used outdoors or in unheated buildings?

CabinPulse is intended to be used indoors only, but can be used in unheated buildings. Note that it is rated to continue functioning from -20°C to 60°C (-4°F to 140°F) while plugged into power; its onboard battery life is significantly diminished in cold temperatures.

What's the warranty on the CabinPulse device?

The CabinPulse device comes with a 3-year warranty. If you experience any issues, contact our support team for troubleshooting or a replacement.

Do you offer bulk discounts?

We offer negotiated terms for commercial customers which can include Service Level Agreements, additional support and discounted hardware for large orders.

Where is the CabinPulse device manufactured?

We're proudly based in Saskatchewan, Canada; all of our devices are designed and assembled in Saskatoon.

Where are your servers located?

All data is securely hosted on servers located in Canada.
