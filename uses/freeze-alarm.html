Frequently Asked Questions About CabinPulse's Freeze Alarm Feature

What temperature do pipes freeze at?

Water pipes typically begin to freeze when temperatures drop to around -7Â°C (20Â°F) or below for an extended period. However, pipes in exterior walls or unheated spaces can freeze at higher temperatures. CabinPulse allows you to set custom alert thresholds so you can be notified well before reaching critical freezing temperatures.

Does CabinPulse need WiFi to work as a freeze alarm?

No, WiFi is NOT required. Unlike most freeze alarms that depend on an internet connection, CabinPulse uses cellular technology (LTE with 3G fallback) to transmit temperature data and alerts. This makes it ideal for remote cabins, vacation homes, or any property where internet connectivity is unreliable or unavailable.

How will I receive freeze alerts?

CabinPulse sends instant alerts via both text message and email when temperatures drop below your customized thresholds. You can add multiple contacts to receive alerts, ensuring that someone will always be notified even if you're unavailable.

Will CabinPulse work in very cold environments?

Yes, CabinPulse is designed to function reliably in extreme temperatures from -20Â°C to 60Â°C (-4Â°F to 140Â°F) while plugged into power. The device's temperature sensors remain accurate even in very cold conditions, making it ideal for winter cabin monitoring.

What happens during a power outage?

CabinPulse features a backup battery that keeps the device functioning for up to 3 days during power outages. You'll receive an immediate alert when power is lost, and the device will continue monitoring temperature and sending alerts throughout the outage period.

Where should I place the CabinPulse device for best freeze protection?

For optimal freeze protection, place your CabinPulse device in the coldest area of your property where pipes are located, such as near exterior walls, in basements, or in crawl spaces. If you have multiple vulnerable areas, you can add additional CabinPulse devices to monitor different zones.
